# Creative Challenge: Who Doesn't Sell, Helps Sell! The Power of Argumentation

In this activity, you will follow some simple steps that, in the end, will help create clear and useful prompts using AI.


## Step 1: Define the intention

What I want the AI to do, for whom, and with what expected result.

- **Content type**: A realistic weekly schedule / action plan
- **Audience**: You (a professional who works hybrid, studies online, and wants to exercise more)
- **Goal**: Fit exercise into weekdays without burning out, while working around your husband's evening classes and Friday fatigue


> I want the AI to generate a list of persuasive arguments for me (or for a seller), with the goal of convincing a customer to make a purchase or sign-up decision.


## Step 2: Add context and constraints

- **Product/service:** A productivity app subscription
- **Customer:** A freelancer who struggles with organization
- **Main objection:** "I can already use free tools like Google Calendar"
- **Differentiator:** Automated task prioritization + time tracking


> Consider the following context: the product is a productivity app with a monthly subscription. The customer is a freelancer who already uses free tools. The main objection is "I don't need to pay for something I already have for free." The content must be in the format of 5 main arguments, each with an opening sentence and a rebuttal to the objection. Avoid technical jargon, aggressive tone, and direct comparisons with competitors.



## Step 3: Combine everything into a final prompt

**FINAL PROMPT**

> I want the AI to generate a list of persuasive arguments for a seller, with the goal of convincing a freelancer customer to subscribe to a productivity app. Consider the following context: the product is a productivity app with a monthly subscription. The customer already uses free tools. The main objection is "I don't need to pay for something I already have for free." The content must be in the format of 5 main arguments, each with an opening sentence and a rebuttal to the objection. Avoid technical jargon, aggressive tone, and direct comparisons with competitors.


### Generated Result

**1. Save Time Every Week**

Opening sentence:
*Your time is one of your most valuable business assets, and even small efficiency gains can add up quickly.*

Rebuttal to the objection:
While free tools can handle basic tasks, paying for a dedicated solution can help reduce the time spent organizing work, switching between apps, and managing daily responsibilities. The value often comes from the hours you save, not just the features you use.


**2. Stay Focused on Revenue-Generating Work**

Opening sentence:
*The more time you spend on client work, the more opportunities you have to grow your income.*

Rebuttal to the objection:
Free tools may cover the essentials, but a structured productivity system can help you spend less energy on administrative tasks and more on activities that directly contribute to your business goals.


**3. Reduce Stress and Mental Overload**

Opening sentence:
*Having a single place to organize tasks and priorities can make work feel more manageable and less overwhelming.*

Rebuttal to the objection:
Even if free alternatives are available, many freelancers find that investing in a tool they rely on daily helps create consistency and peace of mind, making it easier to stay organized when workloads increase.



**4. Build Better Work Habits**

Opening sentence:
*Long-term success often comes from maintaining productive routines rather than working harder.*

Rebuttal to the objection:
The question is not whether free tools exist, but whether your current setup consistently supports your goals. A paid solution can serve as a commitment to a more organized and intentional way of working.


**5. View It as a Business Investment**

Opening sentence:
*Every business expense should be evaluated based on the value it creates, not just its cost.*

Rebuttal to the objection:
If a productivity app helps you stay organized, meet deadlines more reliably, and free up even a small amount of time each month, the return can outweigh the subscription fee. The focus is not on replacing free tools, but on supporting your professional growth more effectively.
